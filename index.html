Key Trade-offs & Risks

Trade-off: Accuracy vs. Speed-to-Market

A simple time-series model (e.g., day-of-week averages with trend adjustment) will be less accurate than a full ML pipeline â€” but it ships in weeks and immediately adds value. Recommendation: Launch Phase 1 with a lightweight model, display confidence ranges, and iterate with more sophisticated ML as data accumulates. Seller trust is built by being directionally right, not perfectly precise.

Risk: New Sellers with Limited Data

Sellers with fewer than 90 days of history won't have strong forecast signals. Mitigation: Gate the feature behind a data threshold, show a progress indicator ("14 more days until your forecast unlocks"), and use anonymized cohort-level benchmarks for new sellers in the interim.

Trade-off: Platform Feature vs. Tier-gated Upsell

Smart Forecasts could drive upgrades from Free to Plus/Premium. Recommendation: Offer the basic revenue forecast on all tiers (increases platform stickiness across the board) and gate staffing recommendations and inventory alerts behind Plus/Premium. This aligns with Square's stated strategy of expanding software adoption to increase seller retention.
